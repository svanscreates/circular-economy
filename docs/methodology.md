# Research Methodology

## Approach

This research follows a **qualitative and exploratory** methodology, appropriate for investigating an emerging phenomenon where established theory is limited and contextual factors (India's regulatory landscape, informal sector dynamics) are critical.

## Data Collection

### Secondary Data Synthesis

Over **80 sources** were synthesised across four literature streams:

1. **Academic literature** — Peer-reviewed journals on AI in manufacturing, circular economy theory, waste management systems
2. **Policy documents** — CPCB guidelines, Plastic Waste Management Rules, EPR frameworks, state-level waste management policies
3. **Industry reports** — Ellen MacArthur Foundation, McKinsey Global Institute, FICCI packaging sector reports
4. **Case studies** — Published accounts of AI deployment in FMCG packaging and waste management contexts

### Case Study Analysis

Selected case studies of AI deployment in FMCG packaging operations examined through the lens of:
- Technology capability assessment
- Circular economy outcome measurement
- Governance and institutional analysis
- Informal sector impact evaluation

## Analytical Framework

The analysis employs an **integrative framework** approach:

1. **AI capability mapping** — Systematic categorisation of AI technologies by function and maturity
2. **Lifecycle stage alignment** — Mapping capabilities to specific packaging lifecycle intervention points
3. **Configuration analysis** — Identifying viable combinations of AI deployment and circular outcomes
4. **Governance tension identification** — Analysing conflicts between technology optimisation, regulatory compliance, and informal sector livelihoods

## Limitations

- **Secondary data only** — No primary data collection from FMCG companies or waste sector actors
- **India-specific** — Findings may not generalise to other emerging market contexts without adaptation
- **Exploratory** — Framework requires empirical validation through quantitative studies and pilot deployment
- **Temporal** — The regulatory landscape (particularly EPR) is evolving rapidly; findings are time-bound
