# Framework Documentation

## AI Capability Clusters

### Cluster 1: Design Simulation

AI-powered material selection and packaging design optimisation. Uses simulation to test recyclability, weight reduction, and mono-material alternatives before physical prototyping.

**Key Technologies**: Generative design algorithms, material property databases, lifecycle assessment (LCA) integration

**Circular Outcome**: Design-for-recyclability at source — the highest-leverage intervention point

### Cluster 2: Process Optimisation

Machine learning models that optimise production parameters (temperature, pressure, speed) to minimise material waste, energy consumption, and defect rates during packaging manufacturing.

**Key Technologies**: Process control ML, sensor fusion, real-time quality monitoring

**Circular Outcome**: Reduced virgin material consumption, lower production waste

### Cluster 3: Diagnostic Analytics

Computer vision and sensor-based systems for contamination detection, material identification, and automated quality sorting at collection and recycling facilities.

**Key Technologies**: Convolutional neural networks (CNN), hyperspectral imaging, NIR spectroscopy integration

**Circular Outcome**: Higher-purity recycled material streams, reduced landfill leakage

### Cluster 4: Traceability

End-to-end material passport systems tracking packaging from production through consumption, collection, and recycling. Enables EPR compliance documentation and circular supply chain transparency.

**Key Technologies**: IoT sensors, blockchain/distributed ledger, QR/RFID tagging, platform APIs

**Circular Outcome**: Verifiable recycling rates, EPR compliance, brand accountability

## Lifecycle Stage Mapping

### Stage 1: Design
- Material selection for recyclability
- Weight optimisation (lightweighting)
- Mono-material design principles

### Stage 2: Production
- Manufacturing yield optimisation
- Energy efficiency improvement
- Quality control and defect minimisation

### Stage 3: Consumption
- Consumer engagement for proper disposal
- Smart packaging with disposal instructions
- Refill/reuse system enablement

### Stage 4: Collection & Sorting
- Route optimisation for waste collection
- Demand prediction for collection scheduling
- Automated sorting by material type and contamination level

### Stage 5: Recycling
- Process optimisation for recycled material quality
- Contamination monitoring and management
- Market-grade output specification
